Why This Tour Is So Special

Every year between July and October, hundreds of humpback whale mothers travel over 8,000 kilometers from the icy waters of Antarctica to the warm, protected bays of Bahía Málaga. They come to mate, give birth, and raise their calves in one of the most biodiverse marine regions in the world.

Is This Private or Group?

This is a private experience in the sense that you book with your own group (or solo) and are guided personally. However, some logistics involve shared spaces — especially the boat, which works like local transport in the region. That's part of the magic: you may share the ride with whale lovers from across the globe.

Full Itinerary

  • Pickup in Cali (Early Morning) — 3-hour drive through the western Andes to Buenaventura.
  • Speedboat to the Pacific Coast (1 hour) — From the tourist port, board a fast boat to Bahía Málaga National Park.
  • Whale Watching Tour (1 hour) — Board a whale-watching boat with a certified local captain. Witness humpback whales breaching, tail-slapping, and nurturing their calves.
  • Beach Free Time (1 hour) — Relax on a tropical beach. Swim, nap, snack, or enjoy the sound of the waves.
  • Return Boat to Port (1 hour) — Back across the sea to Buenaventura.
  • Return to Cali (Evening) — Arrive in Cali after a 3-hour drive.

Choose Your Own Adventure

Route A — Cultural & Community-Based: Meet locals in a nearby Afro-Colombian village, taste traditional snacks, support community tourism.

Route B — Wild & Untouched Nature: Venture deeper into Bahía Málaga's raw coast. No tourism infrastructure — just you, nature, and the whales offshore.

Due to distances and boat logistics, you can only choose one route per day.

What to Bring

  • Sandals or water shoes

  • Motion sickness pills (optional)

  • Light snacks or fruit

Is Cali Colombia safe?

When people hear you're coming to Cali, they'll probably warn you it's dangerous. Despite its reputation, more and more tourists visit every year and have an amazing time. While Cali may not meet the safety standards of Europe or North America, it turns out it's not as terrible as it seems.

Want to walk around safely guarded by a local?

Click here to book a tour with Juan

FIRST, SOME DATA.

Opinions open conversations to a million ways of interpretation. Facts however, present a more objective comparison point. Here are some interesting facts from last decade:

  • Murder rate in Cali has decreased 63% since the 90's during the height of Cartels era (1994 vs 2023)
  • Automobile theft in Cali decreased 68.3% from 2021 to 2022.
  • Cali presents the lowest number of robberies & cellphone thefts compared to Bogotá & Medellín.
  • There are 5 cities in the USA with a higher murder rate than Cali (New Orleans, Baltimore, Detroit, Memphis, Cleveland).
  • There are 13 cities in Mexico with a higher murder rate than Cali (including Cancún and Acapulco).
  • Regarding cities in Colombia, Cali is NOT in the top 5 for: homicides rate, bodily injuries rate, thefts rate, residential burglaries rate, threats rate, extortions rate.

UNSAFE AREAS TO AVOID

This is a common question with an easy answer. The deep east of the city is the area that you most want to avoid, as well as some neighborhoods up in the mountains. Truth is, you should find no reason to go there, and it's not like you have to go across them to get to interesting areas of the city. Most of the city's touristy and popular areas are safe and walkable, places where you will most probably have a safe and fun time.

HOW TO BEHAVE

Colombians take pride on being kind and cordial, and Cali is no exception. Here is my advice on how to get to be liked (and how not to upset) Cali people:

  • Greet. Colombians love to greet. Hola, buenos días, buenas tardes, buenas noches & hasta luego will go a long way. Plus, we love when you try to speak spanish!
  • Americans: please don't smash car doors. Cars here are smaller and flimsy, specially compared to american cars. When getting into a taxi or uber, your muscle memory will kick in. So just take it easy, do it slow, and don't slam the door!
  • When riding an Uber, ride upfront. The driver will appreciate it as Uber service is still not entirely legal.
  • Uber seems to be the cheapest ride app in Cali. However other options are: Didi, Cabify, InDriver, WayCali, Yango.
  • How to get a colombian upset? Reject their food. Just accept it and try to enjoy it.
  • We're not very straightforward. If we don't like something, we may never tell you.

WEATHER & CLOTHING

Weather in Colombia is not controlled by time, but by altitude. The weather is almost the same, all year round. Cali is an eternal warm: it doesn't ever get too hot, it doesn't ever get cold.

We have 2 notorious rainy seasons that go from March to June and then again from September to December. We are a tropical city: if it rains, find cover. Good thing is that rain refreshes the city. Also have in mind it may rain by cloud: 5 blocks away it may be completely dry. Also, the faster it starts, the faster it ends.

For clothing keep it light, shorts are fine but you will see most local wear jeans or pants and that's mainly for the amount of bugs and insects in the city, specially during rainy seasons.

Avoid going out at noon and at times when the sun is hot and shiny. You will see the city is almost deserted during the day and everyone starts coming out at night.

Now, if you're going out to enjoy nightlife, please have in mind Cali clubs have a high dresscode in general. As a foreigner you will have the upper hand, but you will be sent back home if wearing tank tops, sandals and/or shorts when trying to enter a club. No we don't care it's hot. For you it's summer weather, but for us it's every day of our lives. Weather is never an excuse to wear summer clothes to a club in Cali.

Even when out enjoying nightlife, keep it low profile: no jewelry, no expensive tech devices. Feel free however to wear all your beautiful shirts, pants, shoes, high-heels and dresses.

"DON'T GIVE PAPAYA"

A phrase used in all of Colombia that in general terms means: don't put yourself at risk. Here are my tips and recommendations to enjoy Cali, risk-free:

  • When going out to explore the city: if ever in doubt, dress down.
  • Don't wear expensive jewelry or technological devices.
  • If it's too lonely, specially after dark, avoid walking alone.
  • Take Ubers after dark. Avoid taking taxis in the street.
  • Take 9pm as a safe limit to be out during the week. 10pm max. Midnight is pushing it. For the weekend midnight is fine.
