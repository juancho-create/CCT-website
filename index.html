Why This Tour Is So Special

Every year between July and October, hundreds of humpback whale mothers travel over 8,000 kilometers from the icy waters of Antarctica to the warm, protected bays of Bahía Málaga. They come to mate, give birth, and raise their calves in one of the most biodiverse marine regions in the world.

Is This Private or Group?

This is a private experience in the sense that you book with your own group (or solo) and are guided personally. However, some logistics involve shared spaces — especially the boat, which works like local transport in the region. That's part of the magic: you may share the ride with whale lovers from across the globe.

Full Itinerary

  • Pickup in Cali (Early Morning) — 3-hour drive through the western Andes to Buenaventura.
  • Speedboat to the Pacific Coast (1 hour) — From the tourist port, board a fast boat to Bahía Málaga National Park.
  • Whale Watching Tour (1 hour) — Board a whale-watching boat with a certified local captain. Witness humpback whales breaching, tail-slapping, and nurturing their calves.
  • Beach Free Time (1 hour) — Relax on a tropical beach. Swim, nap, snack, or enjoy the sound of the waves.
  • Return Boat to Port (1 hour) — Back across the sea to Buenaventura.
  • Return to Cali (Evening) — Arrive in Cali after a 3-hour drive.

Choose Your Own Adventure

Route A — Cultural & Community-Based: Meet locals in a nearby Afro-Colombian village, taste traditional snacks, support community tourism.

Route B — Wild & Untouched Nature: Venture deeper into Bahía Málaga's raw coast. No tourism infrastructure — just you, nature, and the whales offshore.

Due to distances and boat logistics, you can only choose one route per day.

Safety in Cali, Colombia

A practical guide for tourists visiting Cali

Good News First

Cali is much safer now than its reputation suggests. The city has transformed dramatically in recent years, and most tourists have wonderful, incident-free visits. Like any big city, common sense goes a long way!

Safe Areas in Cali

These neighborhoods are generally safe for tourists and have a strong police presence:

  • San Antonio — Historic, bohemian neighborhood with colorful streets, great restaurants, and a relaxed vibe
  • El Peñón — Upscale area with parks, cafes, and boutique shops
  • Granada — Modern district with excellent restaurants and nightlife
  • Parque del Perro — Trendy area popular with locals and visitors alike
  • Boulevard del Río — The renovated riverside area is safe during the day and early evening

Areas to Avoid

As a tourist, it's best to avoid: Aguablanca district, parts of the eastern hills (lomas orientales) without a local guide, and any unfamiliar neighborhoods at night. When in doubt, ask Juan!

General Safety Tips

  • Don't flash expensive jewelry, cameras, or large amounts of cash
  • Keep your phone in your pocket when not using it
  • Use a crossbody bag and keep it in front of you
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
  • Don't walk alone late at night — use Uber or InDriver
  • Make copies of your passport and keep them separate from the original
  • Use ATMs inside banks or shopping malls during daylight hours

Transport Safety

  • Use Uber or InDriver — These are the safest options for getting around
  • Avoid motorcycles (motos) — These are the highest risk for theft
  • Yellow taxis — Generally safe from established taxi stands or called by phone
  • MIO (bus system) — Safe during the day, avoid during rush hour if you're carrying valuables

Nightlife Safety

  • Stick to well-known areas like Granada and Parque del Perro
  • Don't accept drinks from strangers
  • Keep an eye on your drink at all times
  • Go out in groups when possible
  • Arrange your ride home before you go out

What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

  • Stay calm — Panic makes everything worse
  • Contact Juan immediately — He knows the city and can help
  • For theft: File a police report (denuncia) — you'll need it for insurance
  • For medical issues: Go to a private clinic like Imbanaco or Valle del Lili
  • Lost passport: Contact your embassy in Bogotá

Local Information — Your Cali Guide

Everything you need to know for your visit

Best Time to Visit

Cali is great year-round! The best months are February and June — less rain, perfect temperatures. December is festive but crowded. Avoid October (heaviest rains).

Getting Around

Uber & InDriver are your best bets — safe and affordable. The MIO bus system works well during the day. Walking is great in San Antonio and El Peñón.

Money & Payments

The currency is Colombian Pesos (COP). USD are accepted in many places but you'll get better rates with pesos. ATMs are everywhere — use ones inside banks or malls.

Food & Drink Culture

Cali is a food lover's paradise! Must-tries: sancocho (hearty soup), empanadas, pandebono (cheese bread), champús, and lulada. Lunch is the big meal of the day.

Weather

Cali is warm and tropical year-round (avg 24°C / 75°F). Mornings are sunny, afternoons can bring brief rain showers. Pack light clothes, a light jacket for evenings, and sunscreen!

Neighborhoods Overview

San Antonio: Historic, artistic
Granada: Modern, upscale
El Peñón: Parks and cafes
Centro: Downtown, museums
San Fernando: Local vibe

Useful Spanish Phrases

"Buenas" = Hello
"Gracias" = Thank you
"¿Cuánto cuesta?" = How much?
"¿Dónde está...?" = Where is...?
"La cuenta, por favor" = The bill, please

SIM Cards & Internet

Get a local SIM at the airport or any Claro, Movistar, or Tigo store. Plans are cheap (~$10-15 USD for data). Most cafes and restaurants have free WiFi.

Health & Vaccines

No special vaccines required for Cali. Bring mosquito repellent. Tap water is technically safe but most people drink bottled. Travel insurance is highly recommended.

Currency Exchange Tips

Exchange rates at airports are terrible. Use ATMs for the best rates, or exchange at reputable casas de cambio in the city center. Always count your money!
